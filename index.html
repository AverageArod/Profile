Timeline & achievements
  • May 20, 1935 José Alberto "Pepe" Mujica Cordano was born in Montevideo, Uruguay to His father, Demetrio Mujica and his mother, Lucy Cordano.
  • 1966 Mujica joined the MLN-Tumpamaros, an armed revolutionary group. Three years later, he led a squad of MLN guerillas in the mission to take over the city of Pando.
  • 1971 He was arrested for his involvement in revolutionary activities but escaped from prison. After escaping several times, Mujica spent the next 13 years in prison. While imprisoned he faced numerous atrocities and at one point he was held in a watering trough. He also spent several years in solitary confinement.
  • 1985 a new amnesty law freed thousands of prisoners. Mujica was among the liberated prisoners.
  • 1989 he founded the ‘Movement of Popular Participation’ (MPP) political party. He soon rose through the ranks.
  • 1994 he successfully stood for election as a deputy. He was just one of two MPP members to win their election. Five years later, was successfully elected as a senator.
  • 2008 the ‘Broad Front’, a coalition of several political parties, agreed on Mujica as their candidate for president.
  • 2012 he was nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize. At the end of the year, he began another term as ‘president pro tempore’ of Mercosur.
  • 2013 his government formally legalized state-controlled sales of marijuana. Same-sex marriage was also legalized at this time.
  • 2015 Mujica's term in office came to an end. He has now retired to private live where he plans to grow chrysanthemums.
